I've been meaning to write up my thoughts about this for a while, and this article on Cricinfo reminded me to quickly note down my thoughts.

Simply put; batting orders are an anachronistic carry-over from "unlimited over" cricket, and don't make sense in modern 20/40/50 over cricket. The advantage a batsman gets psychologically from knowing when they will be going out to bat is lost when they might be batting at 4-1 or 104-1. It takes a very special player to be able to bat equally effectively in both situations.

Currently teams tinker with their batting lineup, and sometimes send lower-order players out when big hitting is required, but this (it appears to me) is done in a very ad hoc fashion. My proposal is that a batsman's position be defined by overs remaining.
